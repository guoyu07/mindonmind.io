RESEARCH SUMMARY

I use fMRI and behavioral tests to study the neural basis of object recognition and categorization, with a special interest in faces. I firstly studied the characteristics and individual differences of face-selective regions, by creating a probabilistic atlas for them. Based on this work, I further studied the function of these regions by investigating the relationship of different neural measures – category selectivity, multivarite pattern dissimilarity, and representational geometry – of these regions, and how these neural measures relate to behavioral abilities.

MAJOR PROJECTS

Details about each project can be found by clicking on its title.

2012-2014   Probabilistic Atlas of Six Face-selective Regions
   Based on a large fMRI dataset and an efficient region labeling software (FreeROI) we developed, we delineated six face-selective regions on the ventral pathway of 202 subjects. A probabilistic atlas was created for the six regions, and different aspects of these regions were further characterized to quantify individual differences and hemisphere asymmetries. Roles 1.Statistical processing of the fMRI dataset by FSL and scripts
2.One of the two core developers of FreeROI
3.Delineation of about 1000 subject-specific regions
4.Atlas construction and most further analysis
5.Part of manuscript preparation

2013-present   Differential Roles of Category Selectivity and Multivariate Patterns Underlying Facial Expression and Identity Recognition
   Using subject-specific ROI analysis and face-related behavioral tests, we found a double dissociation between univariate and multivariate neural measures: face selectivity in posterior STS predicted facial expression recognition, but not facial identity recognition, while pattern dissimilarity in the same region predicted facial identity recognition, but not expression recognition. Roles 1.Project designer
2.Data miner
3.Manuscript preparation
